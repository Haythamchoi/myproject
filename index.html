In this article, a life living as Zainichi in Japan has been well demonstrated. For instance, a lady Chung was born in Japan and she is fluent in speaking Japanese just like other typical japanese people. However, she was not considered as an actual Japanese citizenbut rather she was considered as a foreigner. Although Japanese society changed its attitude towards Zainichi and due to this fact, many Zainichi took Japanese names and became a citizen. However, not everyone. According to Onishi (2005), "Japan has softened its attitudes toward the Zainichi, and many have become citizens and taken Japanese names . Others have taken citizenship, but kept their Korean names. Others, like Chung, have taken neither citizenship nor name".



This academic article written by Arvydas Kumpis "representations of Zainichi Koreans in Japanese media" is focused on the study of living as a Korean in Japan. While the reading was being analyzed, I discovered the fact that surprisingly, Koreans have been considered uneducated and lower class in the Japanese society and this is because the number of Koreans were too few that they were not enough to perform a crucial role in the Japanese society. It seems like that Koreans were highly discriminated based on the historical fact.
